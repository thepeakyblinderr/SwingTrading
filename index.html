📖 Momentum Swing Trading — Beginner Playbook
1
Find the Trend
Price must be above 20 EMA and 50 EMA on daily chart. Never buy a falling stock — trade with the trend.
2
Check RSI Momentum
RSI (14) between 55–70 is the sweet spot. Above 70 = overbought. Below 50 = weak. Avoid both extremes.
3
Confirm with Volume
Volume should be 1.5x+ the 20-day average on breakout day. Low volume breakouts fail.
4
Set Entry, SL & Target
Buy on breakout. SL below recent swing low. Target = 2x–3x the SL distance. Minimum 1:2 R:R.
5
Trail & Exit
At 50% of target → move SL to breakeven. Exit at target OR RSI below 50 OR close below 20 EMA.
📊 Understanding the Score
RSI 55–70  Momentum without being overbought (+20 pts)
Above 20 EMA  Short-term trend is up (+20 pts)
Above 50 EMA  Medium-term trend is up (+20 pts)
Vol Spike 1.5x+  Breakout with volume confirmation (+20 pts)
Near 52W High  Price in breakout zone within 15% of high (+20 pts)
Score 70+ = Check on TradingView before entering.
⚠️ Rules for ₹25K Capital
📌 Risk max ₹500 per trade (2% of capital). Survive 10+ losing trades in a row.
✅ Max 2 open trades at once. Focus beats diversification at ₹25K.
❌ Avoid stocks above ₹2,000/share — can't get adequate quantity.
🎯 Best price range: ₹50–₹800/share for ₹25K.
⏱️ Hold: 5–15 trading days. Review every Friday & Sunday.
📅 Market: NSE 9:15 AM–3:30 PM IST. Best entry: 9:30–10:30 AM or 2–3 PM.

🎯 Entry Triggers — Wait for ONE of these
Breakout
Price closes above a recent resistance level (3–5 day high) with volume 1.5x+. Buy next morning open.
EMA Pullback
Stock dips to 20 EMA on low volume then bounces with a green candle. Buy on the bounce candle.
Gap Up
Stock opens 2%+ above previous close with high volume. Buy after 15 minutes if price holds.
Never buy just because score is 70+. Wait for an actual trigger candle on the chart.
📍 Stop-Loss Placement Guide
🕯️ Candle SL (best): Below the low of the last 3–5 days. Respects market structure.
📉 EMA SL: Below the 20 EMA. Works well for pullback entries.
📏 % SL: Fixed 4–6% below entry. Simple for beginners.
⚠️ If your SL needs to be more than 8% away, the setup is too risky. Skip it.
🚫 When NOT to enter
RSI above 75 — already overbought
Monday after a big Friday gap up
Earnings within 5 days
Budget / RBI policy week
R:R below 1:1.5
Already have 2 open trades
🚪 Exit Rules — Decide BEFORE you enter
3 mandatory exits — no flexibility
SL hit — Exit immediately. Groww auto-sells. Never cancel your SL hoping it recovers.
Close below 20 EMA — Exit next morning open. Momentum broken even if SL not hit.
RSI drops below 50 — Trend reversing. Exit even if you're in profit.
Trailing SL ladder — protect profits
0% Entry → SL at planned level. Risk = ₹500
50% Halfway to target → move SL to entry price. Risk = ₹0 now.
75% 75% of target → move SL to lock in 50% of profit.
100% Target hit → cancel SL, place limit sell. Exit clean.
⏳ What to do if target not reached
Days 1–5: Stock going sideways or slightly down — normal. Hold if price above 20 EMA.
Days 6–10: Still no move — ask: is RSI above 50? Above 20 EMA? If both yes, hold. If no, exit.
Days 11–15: Take partial profit (sell 50%). Let rest run with SL at breakeven.
Day 15+ with no progress: Time stop — exit. Dead capital. Deploy in a fresh setup.
Near target (within 2%): Place limit sell order now. Don't wait — take the planned profit.

⚡ Key Terms — CNC vs MIS vs SL vs SL-M
CNC — Cash & Carry. Hold for days/weeks. Always use this for swings.
MIS — Intraday only. Auto-closes 3:20 PM. Never use for swing trades.
SL — Stop Loss Limit. Trigger + Limit price. May not execute in fast crash.
SL-M — Stop Loss Market. Trigger only. Always executes. Best for liquid midcaps.
T+1 — Shares settle next trading day. You can still sell same day (BTST).
BTST — Buy Today Sell Tomorrow. Allowed in India. Groww handles automatically.

Actual R:R
Every indicator and concept used in this app — explained in plain English. Updated as new features are added.
📊 RSI — Relative Strength Index
What it is: Measures how fast and how much a stock has moved up vs down over 14 days. Scale of 0–100.
How calculated: Compares average gains to average losses over 14 trading days. More up days = higher RSI.
Above 70 = Overbought — stock ran too far, avoid
55–70 = Sweet spot — momentum entry zone ✓
40–55 = Neutral — weak, wait
Below 50 = Exit warning for open trades
In the app: Score capped at 60 if RSI above 70. Trade Monitor warns if RSI drops below 50.
📈 EMA — Exponential Moving Average
What it is: Smoothed average of closing prices, giving more weight to recent days. A curved line on the chart.
20 EMA — last 20 days ≈ 1 month. Short-term trend.
50 EMA — last 50 days ≈ 2.5 months. Medium-term trend.
Price above both = uptrend ✓ safe entry
Price below both = downtrend, score capped at 40
Dip to 20 EMA + bounce = best entry signal
In the app: Both EMAs on chart in orange and blue. Trade Monitor checks 20 EMA weekly.
📦 Volume Spike
What it is: Today's volume vs 20-day average. A spike = unusual participation from big players.
1.5× or more = strong breakout ✓
1.0–1.5× = moderate, acceptable
Below 1× = weak, breakout likely to fail
In the app: Shown as multiplier (e.g. 2.1×) in screener. Volume bars on chart coloured green/red.
📏 ATR — Average True Range
What it is: How much a stock typically moves in a single day, in rupees. The most accurate way to set a stop-loss.
How calculated: True Range each day = biggest of (High−Low), |High−Prev Close|, |Low−Prev Close|. ATR = average of last 14 days.
Why better than % SL: A 5% SL ignores actual volatility. ATR respects how each stock actually moves.
1.5× ATR SL — tighter, for low volatility stocks (ATR% below 2%)
2× ATR SL — wider, for high volatility stocks (ATR% above 4%)
Example: BEL ₹282, ATR ₹11
1.5× ATR SL = ₹282 − ₹16.50 = ₹265.50
Better than guessing 5% = ₹268
ATR% below 2% = low volatility, safer
ATR% 2–4% = medium, normal for midcaps
ATR% above 4% = high volatility, use 2× ATR SL
In the app: ATR column in screener. Chart shows 1.5× and 2× ATR SL levels below the candles.
📌 52-Week High Proximity
What it is: How far current price is from the highest price in the last 52 weeks.
Within 15% = breakout zone ✓ +20 pts
15–30% below = recovery mode, weaker
More than 30% below = beaten down, avoid
Why it matters: Stocks near their 52W high have proven they can reach that level. Breaking through attracts more buyers.
⚖️ Risk:Reward Ratio (R:R)
Formula: R:R = (Target − Entry) ÷ (Entry − SL)
Entry ₹282, SL ₹268, Target ₹312
Risk ₹14 · Reward ₹30 · R:R = 1:2.14
1:2 or better = take the trade ✓
1:1.5 = borderline, adjust or skip
Below 1:1.5 = skip
Key insight: Even with 50% win rate, 1:2 R:R means you profit. Wins make ₹1,000, losses cost ₹500 — you still come out ahead.
In the app: Risk Calculator shows R:R live. Chart shows R:R for suggested levels.
